## Slide 4

THE ANATOMY OF "THE AI SCIENTIST"

Deconstructing the fully automated research framework developed by Sakana AI.


[A circular flowchart shows four gear-shaped nodes connected by arrows in a clockwise loop.]


- Node 1 Idea Generation: LLM queries archives, identifies knowledge gaps, and drafts novel hypotheses.
- Node 2 Experimental Iteration: Agent independently writes code, executes experiments, and visualizes resulting data.
- Node 3 Paper Write-up: AI drafts a full scientific manuscript detailing methodology, results, and conclusions.
- Node 4 Automated Review: LLM-based reviewer evaluates the paper with near-human accuracy, feeding back for the next loop.


Callout Box: Key Insight: Research by Sakana AI (Chris Lu et al., 2024) demonstrates this pipeline successfully generating novel machine learning papers autonomously.

## Slide 11

GOVERNING THE SILICON SEED: TRUST AND VERIFICATION

Technological acceleration risks exacerbating the reproducibility crisis and introducing algorithmic bias into crop science.


PRISMA-AI Framework
Strengths:
- Focuses on reproducibility.
- Mandates AI-specific metadata (model version, retrieval parameters).
- Utilizes verifiable DOIs.
Limitation:
- Risk of over-reliance by researchers.


STARD-AI Framework
Strengths:
- Structured checklists for diagnostic AI.
- Demands strict dataset provenance.
Limitation:
- Confined to diagnostic contexts; requires adaptation for open-ended hypothesis generation.
